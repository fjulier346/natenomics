OCR A-Level Economics – Micro & Macro

Microeconomics (OCR A Level)

Unit 1 — Introduction to Microeconomics

Key ideas: scarcity, choice, opportunity cost, PPCs.

Definition: Opportunity Cost = next best alternative foregone.

Exam Tip: Always explain the opportunity cost in CONTEXT.

Unit 2 — Demand & Supply

Key ideas: shifts vs movements, market equilibrium, elasticity.

Definition: Elasticity = responsiveness of quantity to price/income changes.

Exam Tip: DIAGRAMS = mandatory. Label everything.

Unit 3 — Market Failure

Key ideas: externalities, public goods, monopoly power.

Definition: Negative externality = cost on 3rd party.

Exam Tip: Use real-life examples (pollution, NHS, cigarettes).

Unit 4 — Government Intervention

Key ideas: taxes, subsidies, regulation, price caps/floors.

Exam Tip: Always evaluate government failure.

Macroeconomics (OCR A Level)

Unit 1 — Macroeconomic Indicators

Key ideas: GDP, inflation, unemployment, balance of payments.

Definition: GDP = total value of output in one year.

Exam Tip: Use AD/AS diagrams for everything.

Unit 2 — Aggregate Demand (AD)

Key ideas: C + I + G + (X-M).

Exam Tip: Evaluate using magnitude of shifts & spare capacity.

Unit 3 — Aggregate Supply (AS)

Key ideas: SRAS, LRAS, productivity.

Definition: LRAS = maximum output with full employment.

Exam Tip: Use supply-side policies for long-run analysis.

Unit 4 — Government Policy

Key ideas: fiscal, monetary, supply-side policy.

Exam Tip: Evaluate using time lags, opportunity cost, side effects.

Unit 5 — Trade & Globalisation

Key ideas: comparative advantage, protectionism, exchange rates.

Exam Tip: Always mention winners and losers.
